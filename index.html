About Event

A day for researchers and knowledge enthusiasts to come together.

Language Technologies Research Centre (LTRC) at IIIT, Hyderabad is celebrating its Silver Jubilee (25 years of completion) in a two day event on Dec 6-7, 2024. On this occasion, we are pleased to invite all LTRC family (Students/alum/faculty/staff/collaborators- current and past, well wishers and friends) to the event.

About LTRC

Language Technologies Research Centre (LTRC) was established in October 1999 in response to the preeminent scientific challenge of our time – enabling machines to read, understand and derive meaning from human languages. The fields of application just seem to increase on a daily basis – prediction of diseases based on electronic health records and patient’s own speech; organizations determining what customers are saying about a service or product by identifying and extracting information from sources like social media; analyzing text in emails that flow through Google and Yahoo servers and stopping spam even before they reach your mailbox; help identify fake news; intelligent voice driven interfaces that respond to vocal prompts and do everything like finding a particular location, tell us about weather forecast, suggest the best route to office or turn on lights at home; giving an insight into what is happening and what people are talking about as a valuable information to financial traders; search and select talent; so on and so forth.

LTRC is the largest academic centre of speech and language technology in South Asia. It has four main research labs, which work in synergy with each other. The speech processing lab (SPL) focuses on speech translation from one Indian language to another, develop personal authentication systems for providing secure access to information using biometrics like, voice, video, fingerprint, and so on; and develop systems for content-based information retrieval for non-textual data.

Information Retrieval and Extraction Lab (IREL) focuses on addressing research problems concerning information access, information retrieval and information extraction.

Anusaaraka is a machine translation tool developed by Chinmaya International Foundation, IIIT Hyderabad and University of Hyderabad. It is a software which translates text from one Indian language into another.

Though research in language technologies is only a few decades old, it is already addressing questions about many aspects of human existence. Remarkably, we can now explore human emotions, their reactions in ways that would have been inconceivable a few years ago. These advancements in technology will pave way to new areas as yet unimagined.
